Our Work

Caring for boys in the greater Durban area through education, rehabilitation, and holistic development.

The integral and major objective of our work is to reunite beneficiaries into the community as independent and responsible young men with their rehabilitated parents, families or significant adults. St Theresas CYCC’s aim is ensuring all of our beneficiaries’ fundamental needs and rights are met.

These include:

  • A safe and comfortable environment.
  • A balanced and nutritious diet.
  • Education through affiliation with special needs school, ECD, local primary; secondary and tertiary learning institutions incl. after school programming.
  • Freedom of speech where each beneficiary is provided with a platform to express themselves without judgement.
  • Psychosocial support through individual and group sessions with a Centre-based Social Worker.
  • Committed and caring structure through qualified Child Care Workers who serve as custodians for beneficiaries, providing day to day care, developmental programs, strength base approach and emotional support.
  • Sports and recreation through physical fitness, managing self-control, dance, music, drama, singing, boy brigade’s band and creative play.
  • Spiritual guidance and upliftment.

PROVIDING HOLISTIC CARE

To render a high-quality of therapeutic care in a residential Child Care facility that promote optimal rehabilitative and developmental outcomes. This captures the essence of a century of growth, resilience, and achievement. High levels of unemployment, poverty, crime, domestic violence, substance abuse, sexual abuse and HIV / AIDS are experienced in our region which results in many children seeking access to the empowering programs which our organisation offers.

These include:

  • Creating a safe and therapeutic environment.
  • Ensuring educational needs of children are met through admission into mainstream schools, remedial or specialized teaching for children with learning disabilities.
  • Early Childhood Development program for children aged 4-5 years.
  • Recreational, life-skills and extra-curricular programming.
  • Spiritual Development of children.
  • Collaborative efforts with placement agencies for reunification of families including Department of Social Development.
  • Making optimal use of qualified personnel and dedicated volunteers
  • Establishing partnerships with the corporate sector, like-minded organisations and government departments.
  • Implementing healthy business principles to ensure quality service.

OUR DEMOGRAPHICS

St Theresas Children’s Home has evolved over the years from mainly providing custodial care to children, to new programs established to meet the requirements of the national transformational welfare policy in South Africa. In order to meet the objectives we have developed and a new innovative programmes which provide relevant, effective and efficient services to the children, youth, families and the community that we serve in the greater Durban Area.

We provide a safe and caring home for children who are orphaned and vulnerable, exposed to various form of abuse/ neglect, behaviour challenges, disabilities, chronic illnesses and as well as children who are infected and affected by/ with HIV & AIDS.

ST Theresas accommodates children between the ages of 4 and 18 with the children being allowed to remain with us up until the age of 20 should they be completing their grade 12.

Our current demographics include 63 African children from numerous areas surrounding the Mayville, EThekwini North and South. However because reunification is key to all our programmes, most of the children and youth referred to us come from communities in and around Durban. This number constantly changes as children enter and exit according to their needs and best interests.
